\subsection{Weight Validation: Two-Round Delphi Study}
\label{sec:mrc_delphi}

To provide a structured validation of the author-assigned weights
($w_E{=}0.45$, $w_F{=}0.35$, $w_D{=}0.20$), we conducted a two-round
Delphi study using LLM-simulated expert personas representing seven distinct
professional profiles relevant to PQC migration~\cite{dalkey1963experimental,
saaty1980ahp}. Pairwise importance judgments were elicited using the
Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) nine-point scale~\cite{saaty1980ahp};
geometric-mean aggregation was applied across all seven response matrices to
derive group weights~\cite{aczel1983procedure}.

\begin{mdframed}[backgroundcolor=gray!8,linecolor=gray!60,linewidth=0.6pt,
  innertopmargin=4pt,innerbottommargin=4pt]
\small\textbf{Caveat.}
All expert responses were generated by LLM simulation conditioned on the
stated professional profiles. Results represent a \emph{structured
heuristic consensus}, not empirically collected data.
Label applied throughout: \textbf{LLM-simulated expert panel
($n{=}7$ personas), pending human validation}.
\end{mdframed}

\noindent\textbf{Key Round~1 observations.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Exposure weight broadly confirmed.} The geometric-mean
  aggregate yields $w_E{=}0.459$, within 2\% of the author value (0.45).
  E1 (CISO) and E2 (cryptographer) place Exposure first; E5 (cloud) places
  it highest of all ($w_E{=}0.648$) because at hyperscaler scale the
  harvest-now-decrypt-later threat dominates.

  \item \textbf{Feasibility weight elevated vs.\ author.} The aggregate
  $w_F{=}0.367$ exceeds the author's 0.35. Three domain specialists
  (E3, E4, E6) assign $w_F > 0.58$, reflecting the operational reality that
  in constrained environments (ICS/OT, IoT, Blockchain) feasibility is the
  binding constraint: urgency becomes moot when migration is technically
  impossible within the threat window.

  \item \textbf{Data-sensitivity weight reduced.} The aggregate $w_D{=}0.174$
  is lower than the author's 0.20. Most experts regard data sensitivity as an
  amplifying factor rather than a primary driver; E1 (financial CISO) is the
  main outlier, prioritising $D$ highly due to regulatory obligations on
  long-retention financial records.

  \item \textbf{Aggregated matrix is highly consistent.} $\lambda_{\max}{=}3.003$;
  $\text{CR}{=}0.003 \ll 0.10$. All seven individual matrices satisfy
  $\text{CR} < 0.10$ (range: 0.003–0.046).
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection*{Round~1 Domain Sub-score Validation}

Experts were also asked to review the author-assigned sub-scores $(E,F,D)$
per domain and flag agreements or proposed adjustments with rationale.
Three themes emerged:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{IoT Feasibility is overestimated.} E4 and E2 independently
  argued that $F{=}0.500$ for IoT/Embedded is optimistic; real-world
  constraints (ROM/RAM floors below CRYSTALS-Kyber-512 reference
  implementation on Cortex-M0+) place the effective feasibility closer to
  0.40 for the bulk of the deployed base (-0.10 adjustment proposed).

  \item \textbf{ICS/OT Feasibility is overestimated; Data Sensitivity
  is underestimated.} E3 noted that $F{=}0.500$ still over-states ICS/OT
  agility given 15--25-year equipment lifecycles and change-control
  requirements in safety-certified systems; E3 proposed $F{\approx}0.430$
  (-0.07). E3 and E7 also raised $D$ from 0.500 to 0.550 (+0.05) to
  reflect critical-infrastructure classification requirements.

  \item \textbf{Blockchain Feasibility is overestimated.} E6 argued that
  $F{=}0.400$ ignores the social-consensus dimension of hard-fork
  coordination; a downward revision to $F{\approx}0.300$ (-0.10) is
  warranted for Ethereum-scale networks. E6 also noted that HNDL risk for
  exposed ECDSA public keys on-chain is underweighted, proposing
  $E{=}0.630$ (+0.03).
\end{enumerate}

\noindent\textbf{Key Round~2 observations.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Moderate convergence toward Exposure primacy.} The three

  \item \textbf{Data-sensitivity remains lowest-ranked but stabilised.}
  $w_D$ rose slightly to 0.181 (author: 0.20) as E1's strong $D$-preference

  in sector-specific sensitivity.

  still rank $E$ first and three rank $F$ first—reflecting genuine structural
  difference between domains with high migration flexibility (TLS, VPN) and
  those without (IoT, ICS/OT, Blockchain). This disagreement is domain-
  dependent rather than methodological; the weighted average captures both
  perspectives.

  \item \textbf{Near-perfect aggregated consistency.} $\lambda_{\max}{=}3.004$;
  $\text{CR}{=}0.004 \ll 0.10$.
\end{itemize}

The three contested dimensions share a common driver: all involve environments
where migration is physically constrained by hardware lifecycle or protocol
architecture, and the consensus is that the author sub-scores were calibrated
on best-case migration scenarios rather than median-case deployed base.
Specifically:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{IoT/Embedded $F$: 0.500 $\to$ 0.400.} Four of seven experts
  independently argued that the Cortex-M4 benchmarks cited in the original
  scoring do not represent the sub-M4 majority of the deployed IoT base
  (Cortex-M0/M0+), where CRYSTALS-Kyber-512 requires ROM/stack expansion
  incompatible with existing firmware partitioning.

  \item \textbf{ICS/OT $F$: 0.500 $\to$ 0.430.} The ICS/OT architect (E3) and
  academic expert (E7) concurred that 15--25 year equipment lifecycles, IEC
  62443 change-control constraints, and the absence of PQC-certified safety
  controllers from any major ICS vendor place effective feasibility below
  the author estimate. Additionally, $D$ was raised from 0.500 to 0.550
  to reflect critical-infrastructure classification requirements.

  \item \textbf{Blockchain $F$: 0.400 $\to$ 0.300.} The blockchain specialist
  (E6) argued that consensus-layer migration requires social coordination
  across tens of thousands of independent validators, making $F{=}0.400$
  still unjustifiably optimistic. Hard-fork precedents
  (Ethereum PoS merge, 2022) required 18+ months of coordination for
  a much simpler change. $E$ was raised from 0.600 to 0.630 (+0.03) to
  reflect documented HNDL exposure of ECDSA public keys stored on-chain.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{All six domains are tier-stable} across all four scenarios.
PKI/Certs and VPN/ZTNA remain in Tier~1 (Critical); TLS/Web, IoT/Embedded,

driver of tier assignment is the high-level exposure and deadline profile
of each domain, not the precise weight calibration.

\subsubsection*{Summary}

\begin{mdframed}[backgroundcolor=blue!4,linecolor=blue!40,linewidth=0.6pt,
  innertopmargin=4pt,innerbottommargin=4pt]
\small\textbf{Delphi+AHP Validation: Key Findings.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt,topsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Weights validated.}
  The AHP-derived consensus weights $\hat{\mathbf{w}}{=}(0.471, 0.348, 0.181)$
  confirm the author ordering $w_E{>}w_F{>}w_D$ and lie within 2.1 pp of
  the author values. CR$=0.004 \ll 0.10$.

  \item \textbf{Moderate expert concordance.}
  Kendall's $W{=}0.43$ ($\chi^2{=}6.00$, $p{<}0.05$).
  Universal agreement: $D$ is lowest priority. Persistent 4:3 split on
  E vs.\ F reflects genuine domain heterogeneity.

  \item \textbf{Three contested sub-scores.}
  IoT ($-0.10$), ICS/OT ($-0.07$), and Blockchain ($-0.10$) Feasibility
  scores are judged as overstated. All other sub-scores are agreed or
  require only minor adjustment ($\leq 0.05$).

  \item \textbf{Tier assignments fully robust.}
  All 6/6 domains are tier-stable across all four weight-by-sub-score
  scenarios (T1$\geq$0.75, T2$\geq$0.50). PKI and VPN/ZTNA remain T1;
  TLS, IoT, ICS/OT, and Blockchain remain T2. No tier changes occur.
\end{enumerate}

pending human validation via practitioner survey (planned).}
